\subsection*{Problem 1}
\begin{proof}
   Let $v \in span(v_1,\dots,v_m)^{\perp}$. This means that $v \in span(V_1,\dots,v_m)$ so $<v, v_k> = 0$ for every $k \in \{1,\dots,m\}$.

   If thats the case we have $v \in \{v_1,\dots,v_m\}^{\perp}$ and $(span(v_1,\dots,v_m))^{\perp} \subseteq \{v_1,\dots,v_m\}^{\perp}$. 

   Now let $v \in \{v_1,\dots,v_m\}^{\perp}$ and let $a_1v_1,\dots,a_mv_m \in span(v_1,\dots,v_m)$. We see that, 
$$ <v, a_1v_1,\dots,a_mv_m> = \overline{a_1}<v,v_1> + \dots + \overline{a_m}<v, v_m> = 0 $$ 

This shows that $v \in span(v_1,\dots,v_m)^{\perp}$. This means that $\{v_1,\dots,v_m}^{\perp}\subseteq span(v_1,\dots,v_m)^{\perp}$.

So both the results shows us that they are equal.
\end{proof}


\subsection*{Chapter 7, Problem 1}
We have, 
$$ <(w_1,\dots,w_n), T^{*}(z_1,\dots,z_n)) = <T(w_1,\dots,w_n),(z_1,\dots,z_n)> $$ 
$$ = <(0,w_1,\dots,w_{n-1}), (z_1,\dots,z_n)> $$ 
$$ = w_1z_2 + \dots + w_{n-1}z_n $$ 
$$ = <(w_1,\dots,w_n), (z_2,\dots,z_n,0)> $$ 

Hence we show that $T^{*}(z_1,\dots,z_n) = (z_2,\dots,z_n,0)$
